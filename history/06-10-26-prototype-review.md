V1
### Prototype Overview

- Full patient-facing prototype reviewed end-to-end with live updates made during session
- Claude feedback integrated into an updated version immediately before the call; differences visible side-by-side
- Analytics module excluded from this review (already in dev, needs separate design attention)
- Flows covered: login, sign-up, onboarding, booking, dashboard, post-consult, wearables, data insights, FAQ, profile, settings
- Right-hand (most recent) version used as the basis for all feedback; left-hand was the earlier production link

### Homepage Copy and Feel

- Three headline variants reviewed for the hero section:
  - “More than a health check”: group leaning toward this; says what it is on the tin
  - “Smart prevention built around you”: clearer on intent but less immediately legible to a new visitor
  - “Comprehensive blood test, well data, and 45 minutes”: too literal, doesn’t convey the offer
- Subtitle “A test is one morning, your health isn’t” flagged for rework by Sean
- Consensus: headline decision deferred to a separate copy session; not blocking design progress
- “No hidden fees / cancel anytime” copy flagged as counterproductive
  - “No hidden fees” makes people think something is hidden; “cancel anytime” raises cancellation before they’ve signed up
  - Replace with an explicit inclusions list: state the price (e.g. £320/£160 discounted), then list what’s included
- Trust signals added to the prototype landing page; agreed these should also appear on the main website homepage
- CTA button needs larger font size to stand out above the description text
- Subdomain (patients.evida) should feel visually continuous with the main site
  - Every page on the main site has a hero image behind content; the subdomain breaks from this
  - Agreed fix: keep the top nav consistent across the subdomain so users don’t feel they’ve left the site
  - Background color on subdomain is a lighter cream vs. the main site; worth aligning or deliberately justifying

### Revised Sign-Up and Payment Flow

- Current three-screen flow (account creation, phone verification, payment) to be collapsed into a unified journey
- Core tension debated: pay first vs. book first
  - Paying first risks frustration if the user discovers appointments are weeks away after committing money
  - Booking first sets expectations correctly and means the £320/£160 feels earned before payment
  - Sean: “I don’t want to pay and then find out I can only get an appointment in a month”
  - Agreed framing: happiness = delta between expectations and reality; booking first closes that gap
  - Target users are time-poor but have spending power; once value is clear, they’ll pay; the job of the website is to establish that value before the flow begins
- Proposed new golden path (single unified flow):
  1. Membership/pricing context screen: price, value proposition, inclusions
  2. Single account creation screen: email + phone on one page, both verified together
  3. Blood test booking: clinic selection and time slot (Randox)
  4. GP virtual consultation booking (Simpl integration)
  5. Payment / activate membership, with order summary
  6. Optional onboarding tour, then dashboard
- “Pay later” secondary CTA retained on the payment screen
  - Reworded so it doesn’t imply the account hasn’t been created yet
  - Suggested copy: “Take me to the dashboard” rather than “I’ll pay later, create my account”
- “Book first” added as a secondary CTA on the membership/pricing screen
  - Leads directly into the booking flow before account creation
  - Payment collected at the end of the booking journey instead
- Skip option preserved at the booking step for users who want to pay immediately without booking
  - Dashboard will surface incomplete steps (book blood test, book GP, etc.) with prompts to return
- Slot-holding timeout (currently 15 minutes in Simpl) noted but not a concern for the pilot scale
- Too many divergent journeys flagged as a risk; agreed to optimize for one golden path with skips built in rather than multiple parallel flows

### Booking Flow Details

- Blood test booking (Randox):
  - Flow: clinic selection, then date and time slot
  - Copy to add: 30-minute window, arrive 5 minutes early, results sent directly to Evida (not to the patient)
  - Four things tested: blood test, height, weight, blood pressure; this context should appear on the booking screen
- GP virtual consultation booking (Simpl):
  - 5-day delay from blood test hard-coded for now; can be adjusted
  - July availability likely Tuesdays and Fridays only
  - Copy to add: up to 45 minutes, virtual, with GP (not health coach)
  - Clarify on screen: patient does not receive a copy of blood results directly; GP reviews them together in the consult
- Health questionnaire (medical history form from Simpl):
  - Should be framed as a required pre-consult step, not optional
  - Copy angle: “You have 45 minutes with a GP; don’t spend 15 of them on admin”
  - GPs can capture it live if not completed, but this should not be communicated to users (avoids encouraging skipping)
  - Suggested placement: part of the post-payment onboarding checklist, not embedded in the booking flow itself
  - Reminder to be sent ahead of the GP consult
- Date of birth input: current modal defaults to today’s date, requiring ~34 clicks to reach 1990s birth years
  - Replace with a typed input field; flagged as a known friction point
- Calendar invite: “Add to calendar” already downloads a file; add fasting reminder to the blood test invite
  - Also send a fasting reminder email at 6pm the night before the appointment
- Confirmation/order summary screen:
  - Should include a receipt or reference number for payment records
  - Summarizes both bookings and membership in a single cart-style view before payment
  - Aman noted this is the right design direction: “a proper cart experience”

### Dashboard and Data Insights

- Pre-consult checklist should be front and center on the dashboard, not at the bottom
  - Items: blood test booked, ID verification complete, health questionnaire submitted, wearables connected
  - Gamification: percentage complete indicator, e.g. “You’re 40% ready for your consult”
  - This checklist is the primary thing a new user needs to see; everything else is secondary until it’s done
- Identity verification (photo ID + selfie): currently happens post-setup; agreed it belongs on the onboarding checklist
  - Suggested position: after payment, as one of the checklist items alongside blood test and questionnaire
- Data insights: add small “?” tooltips on individual metrics
  - Example: Vitamin D level shown without context; average user won’t know why it matters or what the number means
  - Some metrics may not need to be shown at all if they require too much explanation
- Universal search bar agreed as worthwhile addition
  - Two distinct mental models in play: search (user knows what they want) vs. chatbot Ask Evi (user has an open question)
  - Both should coexist; they serve different users and are not redundant
  - Search results page will need to be designed; fuzzy matching, one-time backend setup
  - GP side especially benefits: can quickly surface specific patient metrics without clicking through multiple tabs
  - Page-level search also considered for documents specifically; universal search preferred as the broader solution
- Ask Evi chatbot: access to same FAQ content as the FAQ page; click to open (not auto-popup)
  - Agreed that auto-popup chatbots are annoying; opt-in model preferred
- Navigation: dev version replaces sidebar with a different tab structure; design language to be aligned before merging prototype and dev

### Profile, Documents, and Health History

- Health questionnaire content lives in Profile (not only in Documents)
  - Dashboard shows a snapshot that links back to Profile or Documents
  - Questionnaire is editable over time, not a locked document
- Version tracking required for audit and regulatory reasons
  - If a patient edits a field, the previous version must be retained (similar to Git version history)
  - GP view: should show edit history with timestamps so they can ask “it looks like this changed, what happened?”
  - Patient view: show current state only; no need to surface historical versions to the patient
  - Family history unlikely to change; other fields (conditions, medications) may be updated before each annual check
  - Suggested UX: “Please review your medical information” prompt before each new cycle, rather than sending a new form
- Document uploads: current state is a Dropbox-style list; sufficient for MVP
  - Full EMR ingestion (parsing scanned PDFs, x-rays, historical letters) flagged as high-value but post-launch
  - Key blockers: many records are paper-scanned with dates cut off; edge cases are significant and hard to handle reliably
  - X-rays and decades of letters represent a different document class; image library approach may be the right model
  - Agreed path: standardize ingestion for known structured sources (Randox results, Simpl forms) first; tackle unstructured uploads after launch
  - GP-side benefit of ingestion noted: a summarized snapshot of all uploaded documents would improve consult quality
  - Dhruv to assess complexity of ingestion pipeline; Aman’s framing: “upload a file to an LLM, extract structured fields, map to database”

### Support, Comms, and Settings

- Support options discussed; agreed on a layered model:
  - Contact form with topic selector (pill or dropdown) for initial input
  - Ongoing conversation thread lives in Messages (not a separate inbox)
  - 15-minute calendar booking slot for direct access to the team
  - Ask Evi escalation path to a human for async questions (response within 24 hours)
  - Generic support email as a fallback for MVP; Zendesk or equivalent not needed at pilot scale
- Notification and comms settings:
  - SMS reminder one hour before each appointment
  - Email reminders for appointments and pre-consult checklist items
  - Newsletter, promotional, and reminder email opt-in/out toggles to be added in next version
  - Aman to define the full email taxonomy in a separate session before settings are finalized
- Phone number: optional at sign-up; if added later, must be verified at that point
  - Email verification forced; phone optional for now, can be made mandatory if SMS becomes critical
- Account deletion:
  - Must be as easy to delete as it is to sign up (GDPR requirement; regulation cited)
  - Show a clear “danger zone” warning with explicit consequences before confirming deletion
  - Note that data is retained per regulation; link to data privacy policy (CQC policy covers retention requirements)
- No-show policy for Randox appointments: cost falls on Randox, not Evida
  - Rescheduling workflow not yet designed; flagged as a separate workstream

### Open Questions and Post-Launch Workstreams

- GP-side platform: next major design workstream after patient flow is finalized
  - Will reuse as much of the patient-side design as possible
  - Key additions: patient list, appointment panel, deeper analytics access, and document/history view
- Website sub-pages vs. single long scroll: unresolved; personal preference varies
  - “How it works” and “Membership” pages may overlap significantly in content
  - SEO argument for separate pages (each can have its own metadata); UX argument for fewer clicks
  - Agreed to revisit separately; not blocking prototype work
- Pricing and cancellation policy: separate conversation needed before launch copy is finalized
- Slot-holding at scale: 15-minute Simpl timeout noted; not a concern for pilot but needs a policy before scaling
- Whether to force booking before payment or allow payment-first: golden path agreed, but skip option means both journeys exist; monitor drop-off data to optimize
