## 1. Positioning Statement (internal)

Evida is a **human + tech enabled service** — not an app.

The human element (GP-led consultations, lifestyle focus) and the tech element (wearable data, diagnostics, platform) are both load-bearing. Neither alone is the story. The app is part of the service, not the product. The framing is: we are in service of our customers.

---

## 2. The Operational Engine (5-Step Sequence)

The whiteboard captures the true service sequence. This was missed by the Granola notes but is the backbone that all copy should reflect:

```
i.   Blood test          →    The diagnostic input (Randox clinic)
ii.  Wearable data       →    The ambient stream (Apple, Oura, Whoop, Garmin)
iii. Book consult        →    The clinical pivot point (45-min video GP)
iv.  Lifestyle focus /
     GP time             →    The human interpretation layer
v.   Informed action /
     recommendations     →    The output — plan, partner network, Evi
```

Grouped as a framework:

| Stage | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Input (telemetry)** | i. Blood test + ii. Wearable data |
| **Translation (clinical)** | iii. Book consult + iv. Lifestyle-focused GP review |
| **Output (action)** | v. Informed action plan + partner access + ongoing support |

This is the structural replacement for **Track → Tailor → Act**, which the team agreed to move away from. The new three pillars are:

- **Data-led** — bloods, wearables, medical history combined
- **GP-led insights** — unhurried clinical interpretation of that data
- **Action / recommendations** — practical next steps and partner integrations

## 4. Messaging Hierarchy

### Layer 1 — Hook (peaks interest)
> More than a health check.

### Layer 2 — Ephemeral benefit (the outcome)
What the member gets in their life:
- Healthier years
- Improved quality of life

**Preferred subheadline direction:** "Your membership to healthier years"
**Also in the mix:** "Adding healthy years to your life"

Structural question pending: collapse to one strong subheadline + list, or retain a two-line hero structure.

### Layer 3 — Concrete service (what is actually delivered)
- Blood tests (100+ biomarkers, Randox clinic)
- Wearable integration (connects data you already have)
- **90 minutes of core GP time across the year** (45-min baseline + 45-min follow-up)
- Medical history in one place
- Follow-up plan and partner access

### GP Time — Confirmed Breakdown

| Slot | Duration | Marketing status |
|---|---|---|
| Initial baseline consultation | 45 min | **Lead with this** |
| 6-month follow-up | 45 min | **Lead with this** |
| 2 × optional check-in consults | 15 min each | Mention, don't headline |

**Core = 90 minutes** (the two 45-min sessions). This is the number to use in headlines and inclusions copy.

The 2 × 15-min sessions are optional and supplementary — they can appear in the inclusions list but should not be the lead claim. Do not combine all into a single number in marketing copy.

## 5. Core Value Propositions

In priority order based on what resonates in conversation:

| Value prop | What it means | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Wearable integration** | Connect data you already generate; one place for bloods + wearable + history | **Main differentiator and hook.** People light up when this comes up. Still works without a wearable — make the caveat explicit. |
| **Lifestyle-focused GP time** | Unhurried consultation with a doctor oriented toward prevention, not illness | Nobody else is doing this. Don't bury it. |
| **Convenience** | Time saved across all three touchpoints: booking, results, actions | Convenience is not one thing — it has three distinct moments (see below). |
| **Healthier years** | The durable outcome of all of the above | More abstract; needs the concrete layer to earn it. |
| **Trusted partner** | Ongoing support, Evi, follow-ups, not a one-and-done check | Builds after the initial experience; keep in for retention messaging. |

### Convenience — Three Touchpoints

The whiteboard breaks TIME / CONVENIENCE into three distinct branches, not a single tag:

1. **Booking** — easy, fast, no friction
2. **Results** — delivered to one place, not scattered
3. **Actions / Recs** — clear, clinician-authored, not generic

Copy that claims "convenient" needs to demonstrate it at all three moments.

## 6. Differentiators

What Evida does that others don't:

1. **Wearable integration** — connects existing device data to clinical context
2. **Lifestyle-medicine GP time** — 45-minute unhurried consult with prevention focus; rare
3. **Data in one place** — blood panel + wearable + medical history, joined up
4. **Continuity** — not a one-off check; follow-up built in from the start

Not differentiated on: biomarker count alone, AI features, price point.

---

## 7. What Evida Is Not

- **Not an app** — the mobile interface is a communication layer for a human clinical service
- **Not a point-in-time diagnostic kit** — point-in-time test kits leave users stranded with data; Evida is a year-long monitoring partnership
- **Not an NHS competitor** — complementary preventative care that works alongside primary care; make this explicit in FAQs
- **Not an AI doctor** — AI is an internal tool that helps GPs catch subtle trends. Every recommendation comes from a licensed GP. Patients are always seen by a real person.

Three psychological approaches. Use these as raw material — don't finalise without testing.

### Approach A — Aspirational (healthspan focus)
Targets emotional benefit of long-term vitality.

1. Your annual membership to healthier years.
2. More than a health check. A lifelong plan for vitality.
3. Invest in your healthspan. Live better, longer.
4. Don't just track your lifespan. Extend your healthy years.

*Subheadline option:* "Most health data sits scattered across devices, unread and unlinked. Evida unites your baseline biomarkers with daily wearable tracking, giving a dedicated GP the clear picture needed to safeguard your future."

### Approach B — Concrete (data and time focus)
Targets practical buyers who want to know exactly what they're getting.

6. Comprehensive diagnostics meet unhurried GP care.
7. Your biomarkers, wearables, and medical history. Finally in one place.
8. 100+ biomarkers. Continuous data sync. 90 minutes of GP care.
9. The clinical upgrade your wearable data has been waiting for.
10. Complete preventative health management for £320 a year.

*Subheadline option:* "Standard health assessments offer quick check-ins and generic advice. Evida gives you an automated secure data network and dedicated clinical time to keep you ahead of potential illness."

### Approach C — Direct contrast (antidote framing)
Targets people frustrated by rushed, reactive NHS appointments.

11. More than a health check. Because your doctor needs time to listen.
12. Rushed appointments won't protect your health. Evida will.
13. Your wearable spots the trends. We give your GP time to read them.
14. Stop guessing about your health data. Get real clinical direction.
15. A premium healthcare service designed around prevention, not reactions.

*Subheadline option:* "The typical health check lasts ten minutes and only reacts after you get sick. Evida combines technology with deep clinical analysis, giving you the space and insight to stop chronic issues before they appear."

## 9. Page Architecture

### Homepage
**Objective:** Hook, prove authority, drive to membership funnel.

Sections:
1. Hero — "More than a health check" + unified delivery statement + dual CTA
2. Trust bar — CQC / GMC / GDPR badges (already on page)
3. The problem — 81-year lifespan vs ~47 healthy years (already on page, keep)
4. The framework — Data → Insights → Action (replaces Track.Tailor.Act carousel)
5. Product visuals — real dashboard screenshots showing wearable + biomarker data
6. Social proof — replace placeholder testimonials with real member quotes
7. Footer CTA

CTAs: Primary `Book Now` / Secondary `See How It Works`

### Membership Page
**Objective:** Address financial objections, justify £320, clarify inclusions.

Sections:
1. Hero — subheadline in "Your membership to healthier years" direction
2. Core matrix — flat static 3-column (Data / Insights / Action), **no carousel**
3. Pricing block — £320, clear inclusions list, no hidden tiers
4. Comparison — Evida vs one-off check vs NHS GP (already on page, good)
5. FAQ anchor

CTAs: Primary `Activate Membership` / Secondary `View what's included`

### How It Works
**Objective:** Remove execution ambiguity; walk through the actual journey.

Phase-based timeline:
- Days 1–3: Onboarding, account setup, wearable pairing
- Week 1: Randox clinic blood draw
- Week 2: Results delivered; book 45-min GP consultation
- Months 1–6: Follow your plan; Evi daily support
- Month 6: Second 45-min follow-up, progress vs baseline

CTAs: Primary `Start Your Journey`

### About Us
**Objective:** The "why" page — builds trust for investors, stakeholders, and selective consumers.

Reframe away from company page toward:
- Vision: shifting healthcare from reactive to protective
- Clinical governance: medical leadership, GMC registrations, NHS background
- Human-in-the-loop philosophy: AI surfaces trends; GPs interpret them; patients are always seen by a real person

CTAs: Primary `Book Now`

### FAQs *(does not exist yet — Aman + Drew to draft)*
**Objective:** Friction reduction; address specific objections.

Priority question categories:
- What's included (biomarkers, GP time breakdown)
- Wearable compatibility (Oura, Apple Health, Whoop, Garmin)
- How it interacts with NHS GP
- Data security
- What happens if I don't have a wearable

CTAs: Persistent footer `Book Your Baseline`

### Blog
**Objective:** SEO, organic traffic, longevity/lifestyle content.

## 12. Copy Tone

- Service language, not SaaS language
- Premium and trustworthy, not clinical and cold
- Prefer concrete specifics (45 minutes, £320, 72 hours for results) over vague claims
- "Healthier years" over "longevity" — more tangible
- "We are in service of our customers" — write from that posture
